The Way of Openness

Rapscallion is a "Way of Openness" company. The Way of Openness is a method for respectful engagement around high-stakes issues.

Based on the work of Civic Friendship and Charles Randall Paul

Be Honest

Acknowledge your motives and express thoughts without faking it. Deep honesty prompts others to respond the same way, creating real communication.

Be Kind

Kindness builds trust more effectively than other practices. It's not weak or naive—it's an emotional language everyone understands.

Presume Good Will

Don't assume critics are stupid or have evil intentions. Grant that others are clear-thinking and good-hearted unless proven otherwise.

Listen Well

Focus intensely on the person speaking, not on your response. True listening is about understanding, not waiting to speak.

Share the Floor

Allow others equal time to speak, even when you "know" you're right. This acknowledges mutual dignity in conversation.

Acknowledge Differences

Each person has unique history and perspective. Openly acknowledging differences creates trust for real conversation.

Answer Tough Questions

Address difficult questions straightforwardly. This builds strong mutual trust and allows facing offensive issues without taking offense.

Give Credit Where Due

Sincere compliments from unexpected sources can powerfully move hearts toward trust. Admire excellence on "the other side."

Speak Only for Yourself

Don't stereotype others or claim to know what they believe. Ask, don't tell. Speak only for yourself unless authorized otherwise.

Keep Private Things Private

Respect personal dignity by honoring someone's choice to share privately. Being open doesn't require disclosing everything to everyone.

Building Trust Through Openness

The Way of Openness transforms how we engage with difficult conversations. It's not just about talking—it's about truly connecting, building trust, and finding common ground where it seems impossible.

At Rapscallion, we believe these principles are essential for meaningful dialogue in business, investment, and life.
